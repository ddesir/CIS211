The Central Processing Unit and Its Components

The Central Processing Unit

The Central Processing Unit (CPU) is the unit which performs most of the processing in a computer.

The CPU is the heart or brain of the computer. It receives data input, executes instructions, and processes information.

The Primary Components of the CPU

The CPU has two primary components, the ALU, or Arithmetic Logic Unit and the CU, or Control Unit. The CU extracts instructions from the memory, decodes and executes them. The ALU handles arithmetic and logic operations.
