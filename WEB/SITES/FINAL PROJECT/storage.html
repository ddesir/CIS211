Units of Storage

Bit (B)
The smallest unit of data in a computer is called Bit (Binary Digit). A bit has a single binary value, either 0 or 1.
Byte (B)
A unit of data that is eight binary digits long. A byte is the unit most computers use to represent a character such as a letter, number or typographic symbol (for example, “g”, “5”, or “?”).
Nibble
Half a byte.
Kilobyte (KB)
A Kilobyte (kb or Kbyte) is approximately a thousand bytes (actually, 2 to the 10th power, or decimal 1,024 bytes).
Megabyte (MB)
As a measure of computer processor storage and real and virtual memory, a megabyte (abbreviated MB) is 2 to the 20th power byte, or 1,048,576 bytes in decimal notation.
Gigabyte (GB)
A Gigabyte is a measure of computer data storage capacity and is “roughly” a billion bytes. A gigabyte is two to the 30th power, or 1,073,741,824 in decimal notation.
Terabyte (TB)
A Terabyte is a measure of computer storage capacity and is 2 to the 40th power of 1024 gigabytes.
