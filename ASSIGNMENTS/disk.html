Disks

Disk Sector

A sector is the smallest physical unit of storage on the disk. It is almost always 512 bytes.

Cluster

One or more consecutive sectors, in exponents of 2. Such as 1 (2^0), 2 (2^1), 8 (2^3) and so on...

Cylinder

A cylinder is formed while all drive heads are in the same position on the disk on older hard drives.

Capacity

A disk's capacity is the total amount of bytes that a disk is capable of holding. It is equal to 512 x number of sectors x number of cylinders.
